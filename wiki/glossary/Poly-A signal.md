# Poly-A signal

The hexanucleotide sequence **AATAAA** (or close variant ATTAAA) located 10–30 nt upstream of the poly-A cleavage site in eukaryotic pre-mRNA. It recruits the Cleavage and Polyadenylation Specificity Factor (CPSF), triggers cleavage of the pre-mRNA, and primes addition of the poly-A tail (~200 adenosines).

**Role in gene prediction**: marks the 3′ end of the last exon and the mRNA end site. Gene finders like [[genscan|GENSCAN]] include a poly-A signal model as a [[signal-sensors|signal sensor]] to delimit the 3′ UTR boundary and correctly terminate gene structures.

**Prevalence**: found in ~70–80% of human genes; less strictly conserved than the GT-AG rule, making 3′ end prediction harder than splice site prediction.

See [[eukaryotic-gene-structure]], [[signal-sensors]].
