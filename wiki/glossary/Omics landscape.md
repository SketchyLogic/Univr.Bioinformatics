The set of "omics" disciplines that collectively describe the molecular biology of an organism at different levels of abstraction. They form a cascade:

$$\text{Genomics} \rightarrow \text{Transcriptomics} \rightarrow \text{Proteomics} \rightarrow \text{Metabolomics}$$

| Layer | Object of study | Key question |
|-------|----------------|--------------|
| **[[Genomics]]** | The genome (all DNA) | Which genes exist? What is their structure? |
| **Transcriptomics** | The transcriptome (all RNA transcripts) | Which genes are expressed, when, and where? |
| **Proteomics** | The proteome (all proteins) | Which proteins are present and in what quantity? |
| **Metabolomics** | The metabolome (all small molecules) | What metabolites are produced? |

Each layer feeds the next: genomic sequence determines what can be transcribed; transcript levels shape protein abundance; proteins catalyse the reactions producing metabolites. [[Bioinformatics]] research spans all four layers and connects them.

Research using each layer has different orientations: genomics research is oriented toward gene structure/function, transcriptomics toward clinical and biology questions, proteomics toward physiology/pharmacology and food science, and metabolomics and bioinformatics cut across all fields.

# TLDR
Four nested disciplines — Genomics → Transcriptomics → Proteomics → Metabolomics — each studying one molecular layer of a cell; bioinformatics connects them all.
