# Open Reading Frame (ORF)

A continuous stretch of codons beginning with a **start codon (ATG)** and ending with a **stop codon (TAA, TAG, or TGA)**, with no in-frame stop codons in between. ORFs are the primary candidates for protein-coding sequences.

**In prokaryotes**: ~85–90% of the genome is coding; long ORFs (>100 codons) are reliable gene indicators. The [[Shine-Delgarno sequence]] upstream of the ATG further confirms genuine translation start sites. Prokaryotic gene finders exploit ORF length together with codon bias.

**In eukaryotes**: most of the genome is non-coding (~3% coding in humans); ORFs are interrupted by introns at the genomic level. Gene prediction must reconstruct the spliced ORF from exon fragments. A long ORF in genomic DNA does not necessarily indicate a coding gene — it must be confirmed by splice site signals and content sensors. See [[eukaryotic-gene-structure]].
