A summary statistic for a set of sequences (assembly contigs, genes, transcripts) that measures the length at which **half the total assembled bases are contained in sequences of that length or longer**.

**How to compute**:
1. Sort all sequences by length (longest first)
2. Cumulatively sum their lengths
3. N50 = the length of the sequence at which the cumulative sum first reaches 50 % of the total assembly length

**Interpretation**: a higher N50 means the assembly is dominated by longer sequences; it is more informative than the simple mean when the length distribution is skewed (as it usually is).

**Example from *Vitis vinifera* reference dataset**: mean gene length = 1,069.5 bp but N50 = 1,173 bp, indicating that the longer genes contribute disproportionately to total sequence mass.

N50 is used to compare the quality of genome assemblies and the completeness of annotation datasets; it does not measure accuracy.
