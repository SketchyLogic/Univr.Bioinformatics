# Spliceosome

A large ribonucleoprotein (RNP) complex that catalyzes the removal of introns from pre-mRNA and ligates the flanking exons. It is found in all eukaryotes and is absent in prokaryotes.

**Composition**: assembled from five small nuclear RNAs (snRNAs) — U1, U2, U4, U5, U6 — and ~150 associated proteins, forming the **snRNPs** (small nuclear ribonucleoproteins). The catalytic core is RNA-based (the snRNAs themselves perform the chemistry).

**Mechanism** (two-step transesterification):
1. The 2'-OH of the **branch point adenosine** (upstream of the 3' splice site) attacks the 5' splice site → lariat intermediate formed, 5' exon freed.
2. The freed 3'-OH of the 5' exon attacks the 3' splice site → exons joined, lariat intron released and degraded.

**Assembly order**: U1 recognises the 5' splice site → U2 binds the branch point → U4/U6·U5 tri-snRNP joins → rearrangements eject U1 and U4, activating the catalytic core.

**Minor spliceosome (U12-type)**: a parallel, less abundant complex that processes a rare class of AT-AC introns (~0.4% of human introns). Uses U11, U12, U4atac, U6atac, U5.

**Relevance to gene prediction**: splice site signals recognised by [[signal-sensors]] (5' donor GT, 3' acceptor AG — see [[GT-AG rule]]) are the molecular footprint of spliceosome recognition. The [[eukaryotic-gene-structure]] page gives broader context.
