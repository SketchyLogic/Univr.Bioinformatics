A branch of molecular biology that studies the **structure, content, function, and evolution of the genome** of living organisms. It relies heavily on [[Bioinformatics]] for processing and visualizing the enormous quantities of data it produces.

Core genomics tasks:
- DNA extraction and sequencing ([[NGS (Next Generation Sequencing)]])
- Genome assembly (reconstructing a genome from millions of short reads)
- **[[gene annotation]]** (locating and characterizing all functional elements)
- Functional annotation (assigning biological roles to genes)
- [[RNA-Seq]] expression analysis (measuring which genes are active and at what level)
- [[GWAS (Genome-Wide Association Studies)]] (linking genetic variants to traits or diseases)
- [[Variant calling]] (identifying SNPs and structural variants across individuals)

Genomics is the top layer of the [[Omics landscape]] — changes at the genome level propagate through transcriptomics, proteomics, and metabolomics.
