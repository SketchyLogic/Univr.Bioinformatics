A family of massively parallel DNA sequencing technologies that sequence millions of short DNA fragments simultaneously, dramatically reducing the cost and time required compared to first-generation (Sanger) sequencing.

Key properties:
- Produces millions to billions of short reads (typically 50–300 bp per read depending on platform)
- Enables whole-genome sequencing, exome sequencing, RNA-Seq, and ChIP-Seq at scale
- The falling cost of NGS has driven a rapid expansion in the number of sequenced genomes and made genome annotation accessible to individual laboratories

NGS is the enabling technology behind [[RNA-Seq]], [[Expressed Sequence Tag (EST)]] replacement, and re-sequencing studies. The raw reads it produces are the input to genome assembly and [[genome-annotation-pipeline]] workflows.

# TLDR
Massively parallel sequencing that produces millions of short reads cheaply — the technology underpinning modern genomics.
